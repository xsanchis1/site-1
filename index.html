About me

My name is Xavier Sanchis, but everyone calls me Xavi. I was born in Valencia (Spain), on the 10th of July 2002. I have lived most of my life in a small town in the province of Alicante called La Nucia.
I studied Electronic Engineering at the University of Exeter, where I graduated with first class honours in 2023. I have alwas been passionate about engineering in general, perhaps because of my curiosity. As a kid, understanding how everything around me worked, from small gadgets to more complex machines or systems, was something that I really enjoyed. As time went on and I became not so much of a kid, I started to learn about disciplines like electronics and programming where I could start to design my own devices and systems. For me, this opened up a new dimension in my love for engineering, which was the power of creating- the creative (and some could say artistic) aspects that this involved. As time has passed I have become very sensitised to the profound impact and beauty of a very good design, and I seek for examples of them everywhere, in architecture, film, cars and of course, electronics and software.

I have many hobbies. I love sports- I will play any sport you throw at me, but I am specially fond of football and tennis, both of which I started playing at a young age. I also love food, film, nature and anything made with love.

Carreer

I am a recent electronic engineering graduate from the Univeristy of Exeter (2023). Throughout my course I have learned many disciplines in depth, such as analogue and digital electronics, control engineering, microcontroller and embedded software engineering, robotics, communications engineering, electromagnetics, project management and machine learning. Throughout my course I have obtained two Deans Commendations, which are awarded to the top 10% highest scoring students of each department every academic year. I have also obtained the Philip Booth prize, which is awarded to the two students with the best final year individual projects of the department of engineering in the University of Exeter.
